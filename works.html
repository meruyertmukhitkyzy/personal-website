  • My Approach

    I’m inspired by Alysa Liu’s mindset: “You cannot beat someone who is having fun.”

    I tend to do my best work in environments that are fast, challenging, and a little unpredictable.

    I’m drawn to difficult problems and new situations because they force growth. Learning something new, figuring things out under pressure, and improving systems over time is what keeps me engaged.

    For me, progress comes from staying curious and pushing through complexity, not avoiding it. I’m comfortable being out of my depth at first, as long as I’m moving forward and getting better.

  • What Drives Me

    A culture of learning and knowledge sharing
    I value environments where knowledge is actively shared, not siloed. Teams that exchange best practices, discuss what works and what doesn’t, and stay open to new tools and approaches tend to move faster and make better decisions. I naturally contribute to that by sharing insights, structuring information, and helping make knowledge accessible across the team.
    Contribution to the industry, not just competition within it
    I’m drawn to companies that don’t just lead, but elevate the space they operate in. Sharing knowledge, setting standards, and pushing the industry forward creates a stronger environment overall and forces continuous innovation internally.
    Diverse and inclusive teams
    I work best in teams with different perspectives, backgrounds, and ways of thinking. Diversity improves decision-making, challenges assumptions, and leads to more creative and effective outcomes.
